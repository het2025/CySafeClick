Personal OSINT Check

OSINT (Open Source Intelligence) is how hackers gather data about you. Let's see what's out there.

Step 1: Google Yourself

Google is the first tool a scammer uses. Open a private/incognito window and perform these searches.

Why? If your phone number and address are easily found on Google, you are a prime target for targeted phishing attacks (Spear Phishing).

Step 2: Check Truecaller

Truecaller crowd-sources contacts. Even if you never installed it, someone else might have uploaded your name and number.

Risk: Scammers use Truecaller to find your full name. When they call you and use your full name, it builds false trust.

Step 3: Social Media Exposure

Review the public visibility of your social profiles. View your profiles as a "Public" user.

Step 4: Data Broker Sites

Sites like JustDial, Sulekha, and other business directories often list personal numbers if you ever registered a small business or sold something online.
