Aadhaar Safety Guide

Your Aadhaar is your digital identity. Learn how to protect it from misuse.

1. Masked Aadhaar

A Masked Aadhaar hides the first 8 digits of your Aadhaar number, showing only the last 4 digits (e.g., xxxx-xxxx-1234). It is legally valid for identity proof but prevents scammers from knowing your full number.

How to download Masked Aadhaar:

  1. Go to the official UIDAI portal: myaadhaar.uidai.gov.in
  2. Log in using your Aadhaar number and OTP.
  3. Click on "Download Aadhaar".
  4. Check the box that says "Do you want a masked Aadhaar?"
  5. Click "Download".

When to use: Use Masked Aadhaar for hotels, flights, and general ID checks. Only give your full Aadhaar when specifically required by a bank or government service.

2. Biometric Lock

Scammers can clone fingerprints from property registration documents to bypass Aadhaar Enabled Payment System (AePS) and steal money from your bank account. Locking your biometrics prevents this.

How to lock your biometrics:

  1. Log in to myaadhaar.uidai.gov.in
  2. Click on "Lock/Unlock Biometrics".
  3. Follow the on-screen instructions to Lock.
Important: When you need to use your fingerprint (e.g., at a bank or ration shop), you can temporarily "Unlock" it from the same portal. It will automatically lock again after 10 minutes.

3. Check Aadhaar Authentication History

You can see a detailed log of every time your Aadhaar was used for OTP or biometric authentication.

How to check your history:

  1. Log in to myaadhaar.uidai.gov.in
  2. Click on "Authentication History".
  3. Select the date range (up to 6 months) and fetch the records.
  4. Review the list. If you see an authentication you didn't authorize, report it immediately to UIDAI (1947).

4. Virtual ID (VID)

A Virtual ID (VID) is a temporary, revocable 16-digit random number mapped to your Aadhaar. You can give a VID instead of your Aadhaar number. If you feel it's compromised, you can generate a new one.

How to generate a VID:

  1. Log in to myaadhaar.uidai.gov.in
  2. Click on "Generate Virtual ID".
  3. Choose to generate a new VID or retrieve an existing one.

5. eKYC Usage

Sometimes, telecom operators or NBFCs retain access to your eKYC. You can check and revoke these permissions if you no longer use their services.

Check the "Aadhaar Services" section on the UIDAI portal to manage which agencies have done offline or online eKYC with your details.

6. What NOT to Do

  • ✗ Never share your 12-digit Aadhaar number on public platforms, social media, or WhatsApp groups.
  • ✗ Never share the Aadhaar OTP with anyone calling you on the phone.
  • ✗ Never give a blank photocopy of your Aadhaar. Always write "Submitted to [Organization Name] on [Date] for [Purpose]" across the copy.
  • ✗ Never use an internet cafe computer to download your Aadhaar without deleting the file and clearing the browser history afterwards.
